What are Parts of Speech?

Parts of speech are the basic types of words we use to build sentences. Each word plays a role in a sentence.

Noun

A person, place, or thing (e.g. teacher, school, dog)

Pronoun

A word that replaces a noun (e.g. he, she, they)

Verb

An action or state (e.g. run, eat, is)

Adjective

Describes a noun (e.g. happy, tall, green)

Adverb

Describes a verb or adjective (e.g. quickly, very)

Preposition

Shows location or time (e.g. in, on, before)

Conjunction

Connects words or ideas (e.g. and, but, or)

Interjection

A short exclamation (e.g. wow!, hey!, oh!)

Noun

A noun is a word that names a person, place, thing, or idea.

  • Person: teacher, doctor, Maria
  • Place: school, park, New York
  • Thing: book, car, phone
  • Idea: happiness, freedom, love

Pronoun

A pronoun replaces a noun to avoid repetition.

  • Examples: he, she, it, they, we, you
  • "Maria is a teacher. She teaches math."

Verb

A verb expresses an action or a state of being.

  • Action: run, eat, write, build
  • State: is, was, are, be
  • "He runs every morning."

Adjective

An adjective describes a noun.

  • Examples: happy, red, tall, delicious
  • "She wore a blue dress."

Adverb

An adverb describes a verb, adjective, or another adverb.

  • Examples: quickly, very, well, slowly
  • "He drives slowly."

Preposition

A preposition shows the relationship of a noun or pronoun to another word.

  • Examples: in, on, under, before, between
  • "The keys are on the table."

Conjunction

A conjunction connects words, phrases, or clauses.

  • Examples: and, but, or, so, because
  • "She is smart and kind."

Interjection

An interjection is a short word or phrase that expresses emotion.

  • Examples: wow, oh, hey, ouch
  • "Wow! That was amazing!"
