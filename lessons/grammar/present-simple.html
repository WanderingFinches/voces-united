What is the Present Simple?

The Present Simple tense is used to describe:

  • Things that are always true ("The sun rises in the east.")
  • Habits or routines ("I work every day.")
  • General facts or feelings ("She likes pizza.")

How to Form the Present Simple

  • I/You/We/They + base verb → "They play football."
  • He/She/It + verb + s/es → "He plays football." "She watches the game."

Spelling Tips

  • Add -s to most verbs (work → works)
  • Add -es for verbs ending in -sh, -ch, -x, -o (go → goes, watch → watches)
  • Change y → ies after a consonant (study → studies)

Examples

  • I eat breakfast at 8.
  • She goes to work.
  • We live in New York.
  • He plays guitar.

Mini Exercise

Complete each sentence with the correct form of the verb.

  1. I _____ (read) every night.
  2. She _____ (cook) dinner.
  3. They _____ (want) a dog.
  4. He _____ (study) English.
  5. You _____ (go) to the store every day.
  6. My mother _____ (call) me in the evenings.
  7. We _____ (try) a new recipe once a month.
  8. She _____ (like) to listen to music.
  9. The dog _____ (play) in the yard.
  10. The president _____ (live) in the White House.
