What is the Present Simple Tense?

The Present Simple is used for routines, habits, facts, and general truths. It describes actions that happen regularly or things that are always true.

  • I work every day.
  • She eats lunch at noon.
  • The sun rises in the east.

When to Use the Present Simple

  • Daily routines: I brush my teeth every morning.
  • General truths: Water boils at 100°C.
  • Scheduled events: The train leaves at 6 PM.
  • Facts: Birds fly.

Grammar Structure

  • Affirmative: Subject + base verb (+ s/es for he/she/it)
  • Negative: Subject + do/does not + base verb
  • Question: Do/Does + subject + base verb?

Examples:

  • He plays soccer. (affirmative)
  • We don’t play soccer. (negative)
  • Do they play soccer? (question)
